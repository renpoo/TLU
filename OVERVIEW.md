## OVERVIEW OF TLU

A Dynamic Money Flow Analysis System Using Non-Equilibrium Thermodynamics.

### 1. Limitations of Traditional Accounting Models

Traditional double-entry bookkeeping and modern accounting information systems are built upon the balance principle as an absolute axiom (a required starting condition). Because these models require all journal entries to be perfectly consistent before any calculation can begin, it is mathematically difficult to directly extract the underlying reality when records are missing or when the "ledger balance" has been intentionally manipulated to look correct.

### 2. Basic Design of TLU

TLU (Money Energy Flow) redefines accounting data as a flow of energy—analogous to fluids in a pipe or electric charges in a circuit—rather than a collection of static, historical records. The system utilizes standard financial data, such as journal entries and general ledgers exported from ERP or accounting applications, as its primary inputs.

### 3. Mathematical Method: Tensor Projection and Flow Analysis

The core of TLU lies in its ability to aggregate and project vast amounts of transaction data onto low-order tensors. Within this framework, the "debit-credit balance" is reclassified: it is no longer a mandatory rule the system must follow, but an observable output signal used to monitor the integrity of the financial network.

### 4. Anomaly Detection Logic

By applying Kirchhoff’s Current Law (the physical principle that total inflow must equal total outflow at any point) to accounting data, the system identifies any resulting deviation as an anomaly signal, $ \Delta $.When $ \Delta = 0 $: The system is in a "closed" and healthy state, where the axioms of bookkeeping are fully maintained.When $ \Delta \neq 0 $: This indicates "energy dissipation" (friction or leakage). In a business context, this signal highlights specific anomalies such as unrecorded transactions, unauthorized fund outflows, or data entry errors.

### 5. Practical Significance

By removing the requirement for a balanced state from the initial analysis phase, TLU can back-calculate true financial dynamics even from incomplete or "broken" datasets. This enables auditors and management to identify "increased entropy" (economic losses or fraud) as a measurable physical quantity—providing insights that are frequently overlooked by traditional sampling or reconciliation methods.
