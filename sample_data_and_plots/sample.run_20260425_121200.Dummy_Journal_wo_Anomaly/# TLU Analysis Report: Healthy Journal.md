# TLU Analysis Report: Healthy Journal Stream (No Leaks)

Based on the latest batch execution of the dummy journal data **without** the `--sales-leak` and `--purchase-leak` anomalies, here is the interpretation of the `workspace/output_data/`.

## 1. 002_ Forensics (The Nature of Business Volatility)

**File:** `result.002_2_1_filter_macro_forensics.analysis.csv`

Even though we removed the malicious "leaks", the system still raised `anomaly_flag = 1` across most of the timeline, with the `mahalanobis_z_score` spiking to **43.08 at t=7** and **33.07 at t=22**. Why?

* **Interpretation:** This perfectly captures the **inherent volatility of real-world business**. The generator script uses log-normal distributions for sales and random delays (30 to 90 days) for AR collections and AP payments.
* Unlike the rigid Kyoto Traffic grid which is physically bound, a business has "lumpy" cash flows. If by pure random chance, several large accounts receivable are delayed and multiple large accounts payable fall due in the exact same month, the shape of the money flow deviates wildly from the baseline average. The system correctly identifies this as a "mathematical anomaly" (a cash flow shock), even though it was caused by natural statistical variance rather than malicious embezzlement.

## 2. 001_ Thermodynamics (Natural Cashflow Crunches)

**File:** `result.001_1_1_filter_macro_thermodynamics.analysis.csv`

* **Observation:** At `t=8`, the Free Energy ($F$) drops to **-22,177.72**.
* **Interpretation:** Just like the forensics anomaly, this represents a **natural cash flow crunch**. In this specific month, the internal "tax" of running the business (Entropy $\times$ Temperature, representing fixed costs like Payroll and Rent) exceeded the Gross Activity ($U$) generated by sales and collections. The organization had to burn its own structural energy (cash reserves) to survive that month. It recovered immediately at `t=9` ($F$ = +23,912).

## 3. Micro Forensics (Where does the natural stress fall?)

**File:** `result.002_2_2_filter_micro_forensics.analysis.csv`

* **Observation at t=2:** Node 8 (`ACC_Accounts_Receivable`) hits a massive Z-score of **17.78**, and Node 2 (`ACC_Cash`) hits **16.38**.
* **Observation at t=23:** Node 3 (`ACC_Payroll_Exp`) hits **8.57**, and Node 4 (`ACC_Rent_Exp`) hits **4.98**.
* **Interpretation:** At `t=2`, there was likely a massive, randomly generated spike in credit sales (driving AR up) without immediate cash collection, stressing both the AR and Cash nodes. At `t=23`, just like in the leaking scenario, the fixed costs (Payroll and Rent) show up as topological stress points because they must be paid regardless of the fluctuating cash pool.

**Conclusion:**
This "healthy" dataset proves a profound point in organizational dynamics: **Even without malicious intent or broken rules, an organization is constantly subjected to severe topological stress due to the random delays (viscosity) between economic events.**
TLU does not just find "fraud"; it mathematically visualizes the very real "friction" and "cashflow shocks" that managers must navigate every day.
