# Multidimensional Analysis of Stock Trading: The User Perspective

This document outlines the analytical frameworks applied when treating **Users (Traders, Institutions, Algorithms)** as the primary entities within the TLU physics engine. In this perspective, the traders are the actors, and the stocks are merely the vehicles through which they interact.

## 1. Kinematics (Behavioral Profiling)
By mapping a user's transaction history into a physical trajectory, we can classify their true nature and intent.
- **Velocity (Speed):** The frequency of order execution. 
- **Acceleration (Aggression):** How rapidly a user builds or dumps a position. Sudden spikes in acceleration indicate aggressive market orders, FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out), or panic selling.
- **Viscosity (Friction):** Represents the "hold time" or patience of a user. 
  - **Low Viscosity:** High-Frequency Trading (HFT) algorithms or bots operating in frictionless environments.
  - **High Viscosity:** Institutional "buy-and-hold" investors or trapped retail traders unable to liquidate.

## 2. Information Geometry & Forensics (Anomaly Detection)
Treating a user's trading pattern as a probability manifold allows us to mathematically prove deviations in intent.
- **KL Divergence (Behavioral Drift):** Measures how far a user's current trading strategy has drifted from their historical baseline. A sudden divergence often precedes a major strategic shift, account hijacking, or acting on insider information.
- **Z-Score Singularities (Micro-Forensics):** Identifies specific moments when a user's transaction volume or frequency mathematically defies their historical distribution, pinpointing the exact timestamp of anomalous intervention (e.g., an Instigator executing a "Pump").

## 3. Network Topology & Phase Mechanics (Influence & Collusion)
By calculating the "gravity" of a user within the capital flow network, we can unmask their true market power.
- **Eigenvector Centrality:** Identifies the "Whales." A user with high centrality isn't necessarily making the most trades, but they are trading with the most connected nodes, giving them immense gravitational pull over market liquidity.
- **Phase Shift & Synchronization (Collusion):** Detects coordinated attacks. If multiple seemingly independent users exhibit perfectly synchronized trading patterns with zero phase delay (0.0 Phase Drift), it proves artificial synchronization. This is the hallmark of coordinated chat-room pumping or automated swarm algorithms.
